In the NBA there is a salary cap that is made for teams to create fair competition. This requires some thinking from owners and GM's to make a decision of who should be paid on the team and which players deserve the biggest contracts. If teams are found going over the salary cap they end up paying what is called a luxary tax. Which in simple terms is a penalty fee. With this info we can help teams decide how to avoid the luxary tax.

Stadium Fun Facts

States There are 28 states that do not have an NBA team and 4 states that have more than one NBA team (New York, Texas, California, and Florida).

Biggest Chicago's United Center is the largest arena in the NBA with a seating capacity of 20,917.

Years between newest and oldest San Francisco's Chase Center is the newest stadium in the league (2019) whereas New York City's Madison Square Garden is the oldest (1968).

Smallest The New Orleans Smoothie King Center is the smallest arena with a seating capacity of 16,867.
